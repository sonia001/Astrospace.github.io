Interesting Facts about Space:

  1. Space does not begin at a specific altitude above the Earth, but the Kármán line at 100 km is a commonly used definition.
  2. The temperature in the void of space is about −270.45 °C
  3. Space is a hard vacuum, meaning it is a void containing very little matter.
  4. There is no sound in space because molecules are too far apart to transmit sound.
  5. The space between galaxies is not completely empty but has an average of one atom per cubic meter.
  6. There are an estimated 100-400 billion stars in our galaxy, the Milky Way.
  7. The universe is observed to be 13.8 billion years old and has been expanding since its formation in the Big Bang.
  8. In the observable universe there are an estimated 2 trillion (2,000,000,000,000) galaxies.
  9. Spacecraft have visited all the known planets in our solar system.

Solar System facts:

  1. The solar system consists of the Sun, planets, dwarf planets, moons, and numerous smaller objects such as comets and asteroids.
  2. 194 moons, 3,583 comets and 796,289 asteroids have been found in the solar system.
  3. 99.86% of the solar system’s mass is found in the Sun. The majority of the remaining 0.14% is contained within the eight planets.
  4. The combined mass of all the moons in the solar system is less than 0.1% of the Earth’s mass.
  5. The four smaller inner planets, also known as the “terrestrial planets”, are primarily composed of rock and metal.
  6. The four outer planets, also known as the “gas giants”, are substantially larger and more massive than the inner planets.
  7. The two innermost gas giants, Jupiter and Saturn, are the largest of the four outer planets and are composed mainly of hydrogen and helium.

Planets facts:

  1. Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system.
  2. 194 moons, 3,583 comets and 796,289 asteroids have been found in the solar system.
  3. Earth’s atmosphere protects us from meteoroids and radiation from the Sun.
  4. Jupiter has more than double the mass of all the other planets combined.
  5. Saturn has more moons than any other planet in the Solar System.
  6. Uranus has only been visited by a single spacecraft, Voyager 2.
  7. It takes like more than 4 hours for light to reach Neptune from the Sun.
  8. Together the planets make up 0.14% of the solar systems mass, 99% of which is the gas giants (Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune).
  9. With the exception of Neptune and Uranus the other 6 planets can be seen unaided and all 8 are visible with a small telescope or binoculars.

Galaxy facts:

  1. The Milky Way moves through space at a velocity of about 552 kilometres per second (343 miles per second) with respect to the Cosmic Microwave Background radiation.
  2. The Milky Way’s central core contains a supermassive black hole. It is commonly referred to as Sagittarius A*. It contains the mass of about 4.3 million Suns.
  3. Our galaxy will collide with Andromeda Galaxy in about 5 billion years. Some astronomers refer to our two galaxy as a binary system of giant spirals.
